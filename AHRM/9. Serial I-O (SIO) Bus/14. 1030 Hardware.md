## 9.14 1030 Hardware

## Controller

The 1030's controller is an 8050 microcontroller running at a clock rate of 4.032MHz. This is a variant of the 8048 with 4K of internal ROM and 256 bytes of internal RAM. An additional 8K of external ROM is connected to the MCU, but unlike the XF551, it is not used for program storage - it is used for extra data storage.

A substantial portion of the firmware is used to store the downloadable ModemLink and T: handler software for the computer: the entirety of the 8K external ROM and about 60% of the internal 4K ROM.

## Known firmware

There is only one currently known version of firmware. The 4K internal ROM in the 8050 has a CRC32 of 329B1D5B, while the 8K external ROM has a CRC32 of 7ABDB8E7.

The internal firmware is highly similar to the 835 firmware for common features. The main differences are patches for the different hardware connections, added tone dialing support, removal of enable/disable sound support, and SIO command processing.

## Modem hardware

Bell 103 compatible modulation and demodulation is performed by a TMS99532 single-chip Bell 103 compatible modem. It is a 'dumb' modem that has no AT command processing and communicates with the MCU using simple digital signals for serial data and control/sensing. Serial transmission over the modem is similar to the SIO bus, with a low start bit, eight data bits sent int LSB to MSB order, and a high stop bit.

POKEY audio output leaked through the SIO audio in line is amplified and connected to the TMS99532's external audio input (EXI). It is always connected, but the TMS99532 only passes it through to the analog output when the transmitter squelch and the answer tone enable (/ATE) signals are asserted. This disables the normal transmission and conducts only the external audio input. The 1030 does not have the ability to pass audio in the other direction, from the phone line to the computer (the 835 and XM301 do).
